Our Research

Biomimetic “Cactus Spine” with Hierarchical Groove Structure

A research group created their own artificial cactus spines. They focused on testing whether smooth spines or textures spine were the best at condensing and moving water towards the base of the spine. Their research concluded that textured spines were the most efficient at collecting water droplets and moving them towards the base of the spines.

Kyoo Chul-Park’s Fog-Harvesting material

Kyoo Chul-Park, along with a team of other researchers have been working on materials to harness condensation for water harvesting systems. They focused on materials that simultaneously enable rapid growth of water droplets as well as removal. The material consists of microscopic, flat-topped rectangular pillars, with conical ramping, and an additional synthetic material.

National Geographic

National Geographic’s article provided details on fog and the three types of fog. The section of the article on cultures that have historically caught fog for their water supply and modern day fog catchers were used as references for the dewpoint project.

United States Environmental Protection Agency

United States Environmental Protection Agency ‘s article on public water systems was used to gain a better understanding of of public water systems and the requirements of safe drinking water.
