How it works

Each panel is 1 foot by 1 foot with over 3,000 spines arranged in a diamond array. Each cluster is composed of three spines that point outward in three different directions to optimize water collection. The change in diameter in a spines conical structure helps move water towards the base, even when upside down. This kind of movement with water is referred to as the capillary effect. The cacti spines and our spines are also covered in strategically arranged bumps and vertical lacerations. The bumps and vertical lacerations pull the water towards the base and cause the water droplets to form together into larger water droplets.

At the bottom of each spine is an aperture like apparatus mimicking a cactus stomata that opens to allow water in but can also close to prevent heavy rain from entering the interior collection system. Each cluster is connected to a pipe inside the structure that the spines can retract into when not collecting water. The retracting mechanism allows the spines to clean themselves and enables easy transport. The interior pipes connect to a collection basin which in turn can be connected to a storage tank with any necessary filtration.
